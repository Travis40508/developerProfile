Beatle Bargains

Beatle Bargains was my first developer project ever. It started out as a late-night experiment and, five uninterrupted hours later, this emerged. It may be primitive and way too vibrant for any sane person, but it laid my educational foundation for future coding projects. The website can be viewed here.

Guessing Game

This guessing game was a labor of love. My understanding of logic, particularly for loops as they relate to coding really evolved with this app. Additionally, I was able to try and add some entertainment value through a story. For reference, try and take on Number Man yourself and see how you fair!

Tic-Tac-Toe

The Tic-Tac-Toe app really taught me the importance of reusable code. When I sat down to write this, I never dreamed it'd take 600 lines of code to finish. I was even more surprised when I found out that some people managed to do it in under 100 lines! Methods, methods, and more methods. Reusable code is the hallmark of any serious developer, and the lesson was learned the hard way here. For hungry Tic-Tac-Toe players out there, the website can be viewed here.

Weather Ever

Weather Ever was my first experience with server data extraction and implementation into code. I started this project extremely disheartened, but left it astounded and proud as to what I was able to accomplish, all while forging a new-found respect for jQuery! For all of your current weather needs, visit Weather Ever now!

Restaurant Roulette

Restaurant Roulette was where I learned how to work in a team. Learning Firebase, teamwork, and Scrum, as well as some advanced Mapping techniques, my team and I were really proud of what we were able to accomplish within two weeks' time. This app really showed us the fruits of our coding labors, as we'd only been introduced to Android 4 weeks prior.

TekeSports

TekeSports was where I stepped away from the code and took on a more managerial role as Team Lead. Being my Team's Scrum Master, point of contact for various API's, and QA Specialist, I had very-little time to do anything else. It was a learning experience for sure, and one that really allowed me to understand what it means to depend on others for success.
